    Istanbul (Not Constantinople)

    Founded on the sarayburnu promontory about 660 BC as Byzantium, the city now known as Istanbul developed to become one of the most significant cities in history. Istanbul is a major port and the largest city in Turkey.It is a city where two continents Asia and Europe meet. It is also known for their magnificent landmarks especially the Hagia Sophia that is admirable because of its architectural wonder's marbled structure and dreamy interiors.

    Moreover, it was previously named Constantinople before being changed to Istanbul on March 28, in 1930, after the Turkish republic formed from the ashes of the Ottoman Empire, which lost its capital status.

    There is also a song about it by They Might Be Giants called "Istanbul (Not Constantinople)".

    Balloon Fest Cappadocia

    Cappadocia is one of the most perfect places on earth, not only for its beautiful landscape, but also for soaring peacefully in hot air balloons. Held over four days in July at Urgup, the event is completely free with charges. Alongside the fantastic site of hot air balloons filling the skies, the Balloon Fest has a large number of great trade stands, fairground rides and entertainment.

    Cappadocia is also known for being made up of underground cities where you can enjoy your stay in a cave hotel. This is the recommended place of the organizers when you want to ride in a hot air balloon. They are the one who will pick you up in your hotel and of course after you arrived in take-off site, the breakfast is already prepared for you so that you will experience the view in the top of a beautiful sunrise.

    Camel Wrestling Festival

    Camel wrestling isn’t as prevalent across Turkey as it used to be & is now confined mainly to the Aegean region of the nation, along the western and the west seacoast. Each January close to Ephesus, one of Turkey’s most well known archaeological sites, a camel wrestling event takes place. Mostly a couple of male camels, called as bulls, have the chance to battle over a lovely female camel, called as a cow. This is one of the most popular festivals in Turkey

    The camel wrestling festival in Selcuk is one of the most famous festivals and takes place every year in January. Camel wrestling has its origins in the nomadic tribes of Anatolia where camels were a part of daily life. The locals breed and train the male camels specifically for wrestling. These camels must be of a certain type, known as Tulu Camels.

    Cappadox

    Cappadox, a progressive multi-disciplinary cultural festival that brings together a range of international and local contemporary art, music, nature and outdoor programs in the dramatic landscape of Cappadocia in the Central Anatolian province of Nevşehir, will be held between May 18 and 21. The festival is celebrated annually since 2015 amidst the beautiful landscape of Cappadocia. The festival is known for featuring international artists including Mercan Dede, Rhye, Ilhan Ersahin, Acid Pauli, and more! This place offers one of the best food festivals in Turkey. The festival also includes gastronomic treats, with chef Mustafa Otar inviting Cappadox attendees to leave modern cooking methods aside and set forth to the place where everything started. Through the Open Fire in Nature event, in which all ingredients are organic and the entire cooking procedure involves organic materials like ash, soil, wood and rocks, Otar will take guests to the origins of taste.
